Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What data are you specifically collecting?

A: We are collecting: Light Levels, Pressure, Temperature, Humidity, CO2, VOC and Sound Levels. Note that we are simply measuring sound levels and not actually recording any audio.

Q: Do you have University Approval for this?

A: Yes, we have approval from Campus OIT as well as CUPD to place our devices around campus and have them connected to the university wifi.

Q: How are you sending data?

A: All of our data is being sent through a HTTP POST request. This means our data can be openly sniffed and collected. It is sent in the following format: 


curl -X POST -F 'temperature=28.31' -F 'humidity=11.47' -F 'pressure=83438.14' \
-F 'altitude=5457.00' -F 'co2=1110' -F 'voc=108' -F 'light=40.63' \
-F 'sound=108' -F 'button=0' -F 'motion=0' -F 'MAC=AA:BB:CC:DD:EE:GG'

Our site then displays the data in a pretty little graph as you can see here.

Q: Where is this data being collected?

A: Data is being collected and sent to a database located on main campus in the SOC-LAB in the DLC.

Q: Are these devices recording our conversations?

A: No. Each device is equipped with a sound sensor however, this sensor is detecting sound levels and not recording audio.

Q: Are these devices taking pictures?

A: None of the devices are equipped with cameras. Furthermore, our project does not involve monitoring people.
